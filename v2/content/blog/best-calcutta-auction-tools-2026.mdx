## We Tested Every Calcutta Tool So You Don't Have To

Running a Calcutta auction in 2026 shouldn't require a spreadsheet, a group text, and someone's cousin who's "good with Excel." There are actual tools for this now. But which ones are worth using?

We tested every Calcutta auction platform, calculator, and tool we could find. Here's what exists, what each one does well, and where they fall short.

## The Landscape

There are essentially four types of Calcutta tools:

1. **Hosting platforms** — run the live auction online
2. **Strategy calculators** — tell you what teams are worth
3. **All-in-one platforms** — hosting + strategy in one place
4. **Spreadsheet templates** — DIY with Google Sheets or Excel

Let's break down the options.

## Calcutta Time

**What it does:** Live auction hosting with a web-based interface. Commissioners create rooms, participants bid in real time.

**Strengths:**
- Clean hosting interface
- Results tracking after the auction
- Been around for a few years — established

**Weaknesses:**
- No strategy analytics — you're bidding blind
- No odds data, fair values, or probability models
- Separate tool needed for any kind of analysis

**Price:** Free hosting

**Best for:** Groups that just want hosting and don't care about strategy.

## PoolGenius

**What it does:** Strategy analytics and probability calculations for Calcutta auctions. Provides odds-based valuations.

**Strengths:**
- Solid probability models
- Blog content with strategy advice

**Weaknesses:**
- No hosting — analytics only, you still need to run the auction yourself
- Pricing is $39-49 per event
- Limited blog content (2-3 articles)

**Price:** $39-49/event (analytics only)

**Best for:** Data-focused bidders who already have hosting figured out.

## BettorEdge

**What it does:** Free Calcutta calculator that estimates team values based on odds.

**Strengths:**
- Free to use
- Basic fair value calculations
- A few blog articles on Calcutta strategy

**Weaknesses:**
- Calculator only — no hosting
- Limited odds sources
- No live auction integration
- Basic UI

**Price:** Free (calculator only)

**Best for:** Quick sanity check on values before a live auction.

## Auction Pro

**What it does:** Free auction hosting platform with basic commissioner tools.

**Strengths:**
- Free hosting
- Basic session management

**Weaknesses:**
- No strategy or analytics
- Less polished UI
- Smaller user base

**Price:** Free hosting

**Best for:** Simple auctions without strategy needs.

## The Spreadsheet Option

Many Calcutta commissioners still use Google Sheets or Excel. It works, technically, but:

- Someone has to manually update bids (and they always make mistakes)
- No real-time updates — participants are refreshing or waiting for texts
- No timer — "going once, going twice" over a group call
- Results tracking requires manual entry
- No probability data or fair values

Spreadsheets are free and flexible, but the time cost is real. For a 68-team March Madness Calcutta, you're looking at 1-2 hours of commissioner overhead just managing the spreadsheet during the auction.
